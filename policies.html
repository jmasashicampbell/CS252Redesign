CS 252 Class Policies - Fall 2021

Objectives

At the end of this course, students will be able to:

  1. Understand abstract models for computing machines including finite automata, push-down automata, and Turing machines.
  2. Understand common notations for defining languages such as regular expressions and grammars.
  3. Understand what kinds of problems are computable and what kinds are not computable.
  4. Understand what kinds of problems are easy to compute and what kinds are hard to compute.
  5. Think and reason carefully about computation. Write about computation clearly and precisely. Use rigorous methods, representations, and proof techniques.
  6. Apply theoretical concepts to practical problems such as using grammars for parsing programming languages and using finite automata and regular expressions for string searching and pattern matching.

Reading

The textbook for the course is Introduction to the Theory of Computation by Michael Sipser. (ISBN: 978-1-133-18779-0) We will cover much of the text following the provided schedule. Please read the material for a given day prior to that day's lecture.

Exams

Exams consist of a Midterm and a comprehensive Final.

The midterm exam will be available for one or more normal testing days as well as one or more make-up days. You should plan to take the exam on one of the normal testing days.

The make-up days are reserved for situations such as unexpected circumstances, UAC accommodations, those who forget to take the test, or misunderstand the testing schedule.

Points will be deducted from your test score if you take the exam on a make-up day. The point deduction for taking the exam on a make-up day is calculated based on five points per day. The deduction for the first day is five points, the second day is ten points, and so on.

You must take the exam on either a normal day or a make-up day, no exceptions. After the end of the make-up days, the exam is no longer available. You need to plan take the exam on a normal testing day so that if an unexpected circumstance arises or you forget the test or misunderstand the schedule, you will be able to complete the exam on a make-up day.

Cheating

All work you submit for this course must be your own individual work. Do not submit work copied from someone else even if you modify it. Do not allow others to copy your work. If you are caught violating this policy your grade for the course will be an E and you will be reported to the honor code office.

The following are specific examples of violations of this policy (note that this list is not exhaustive):

  • Copying homework or exam answers from another student, from the internet, from an answer key, or from a solutions manual.
  • Allowing someone to copy from your homework or exam answers.
  • Copying solutions or parts of solutions from group-work notes or TA-consultation notes.
